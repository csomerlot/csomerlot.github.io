Cruising Catamaran for Lake Ontario

  1. a trailerable 25'-ish open deck multihull
  2. ample sail plan for light wind
    1. Sloop rigged with Jennnaker (no pole needed)
    2. Pirate-red sails!
  3. 10hp OB in center wet well for propulsion
  4. each hull self-serving for 1 couple with double berth
    1. hinged breeze-way hatches
    2. WC
      1. with composting toilets
      2. foot-pump operated sinks
    3. water tank
    4. fuel tank
  5. stow-away galley and nav/lounge table on deck
  6. deep-V hulls so no daggerboards
  7. kick-up rudders
  8. plywood stitch and glue home construction
  9. can carry 3 or 4 kayaks up on trampoline
  10. we'll call her the Glorious Anne-Marie (because there are 2 hulls! get it?)
  11. she'll probably do 20 knots downwind...
  12. Study Plans:
    1. http://www.sailingcatamarans.com/strider.htm
    2. https://www.duckworksmagazine.com/r/plansindex/multihullsail.htm

Ultralight Trimaran Plans

  1. Can be built in field with minimum of tools, in emergency
    1. Skin on Frame?
  2. Uses kayaks as pontoons (amas)
    1. Uses inflatable bouancy bags
    2. Won't plane, though
  3. Dual skegs
  4. Very shallow draft
  5. Minimize gear, ground tackle, and trimming to keep ultralight to maximize speed
  6. Sloop Rigged with Spinnaker
  7. 20-30 feet
  8. submersible mode
    1. Detach kayaks, demast, flip the boat over and load the trampolines with rocks to create a diving bell using the hull
    2. For hiding the boat while you are out kayaking or shelter from bad storms
    3. pedal-powered bilge pump doubles as air snorkel
