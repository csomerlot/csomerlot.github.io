My Apiary

Summary. Compendium of efforts and notes for things to try in my search for better yields in life. Michael Bush's page remains, for me, the BEST reference and inspiration, though I have found local beekeeper clubs to be invaluable for help and advice.

My goals

  1. organic
  2. low-effort/time
  3. 4 Top Bar hives
  4. 2-4 Lang (traditional) hives
  5. 1 observation hive
  6. 300lb wildflower honey per year
  7. 100lb wax per year
  8. nucs?

Top Bar Hives

  1. A simplified way of beekeeping
  2. design parameters
    1. Kenyan style
    2. smaller frames, to avoid breakage
    3. proper bee space
    4. simple stand, at easily accesible height
      1. handles rest on 2 sawhorses
        1. can handle 2 hives at once
      2. hang from tree?
    5. has proper entrance and ventilation
    6. top bars use triangular comb guide, nailed on
  3. baggie feeding of honey to get them started

Observation hive

  1. clear tubing entrance
  2. jar feeder
  3. 3 standard frames
  4. 3 stacked glass plates for sides
  5. screened top for ventilation
  6. frames put in horizontally, after glass plates have been slid up and out
    1. Can use top bars or frames
  7. groove for light blockers outside of glass grooves
    1. use cardboard
    2. tape "life cycle of bees" posters (brushy mountain bee supply) to cardboard

IPM for mites (in order)

  1. natural cell sizes
    1. takes 2 generations to regress the size of bees
    2. foundationless frames with triangular comb guide nailed on
      1. this leads to comb being built all over the place, for it to work either need frequent checks to encourage correct comb or frames already drawn on either side of empties
    3. do "shakedowns"
  2. hygenic-strain queen replacements
    1. includes feral bees?
  3. screened bottom boards
  4. drone brood culling
  5. mite-away II

Harvesting

  1. double-pass filter system in 5-gallon buckets
  2. cut raw comb from frames and put right into filter bucket
  3. harvest often
