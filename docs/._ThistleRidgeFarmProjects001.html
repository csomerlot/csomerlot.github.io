home made equipment

Drum Carder

  1. motorized
    1. guts from an old electric drill
      1. put trigger into foot pedal
    2. bi-directional
    3. allows 2-handed fiber management
  2. main drum
    1. size
      1. 12" wide by 15.25" diameter
        1. 48" circumference
      2. 576 sq. in.
    2. laminated plywood
  3. roving guide
    1. diz?
    2. doffing netting strip
    3. maybe just skip the whole roving thing
  4. chain driven
    1. front bike cassette sprokets for multiple carding ratios
    2. small tension sproket
      1. motorized
    3. small sproket outside of chain loop for main drum
  5. cloth
    1. 1/2" deep
      1. can use netting for doffing
        1. netting attached at one end under groove guide
    2. ~100 PSI
    3. find a manufacturer who gives samples
      1. too expensive from fiber dealers
      2. need about 50' of 1" tape

Loom

  1. counter-balance style (overhead)
  2. 48"
  3. 4 harnesses
    1. simplest possible to make tartan with
  4. 6 treadles
  5. string heddles
    1. may be better to purchase some steel ones
  6. ball bearings on beater and counter balances
  7. sectional warp winder
    1. counter on warp beam or warp roller
      1. use 18" circumference rollers for ease of counting?
  8. collapsable
  9. uses standard purchased reeds
  10. needs a clever brake system
    1. equal size warp and cloth rollers w/ chain & sprokets?
      1. keeps tension equal throughout cloth
    2. foot brake to adjust tension
  11. References
    1. Paula Simmon's books

References

  1. David Bryant's book "wheels and Looms"

Spinning Wheel

  1. quill spindle style
    1. simplicity of construction
    2. flexibility in yarn type
    3. can also wind shuttle bobbins and make center pull balls
    4. speed
    5. no orifice threading
  2. single treadle
    1. simplicity
    2. dual action
    3. Can be set at any angle from wheel, to adjust position of wheel relative to spinner
  3. Primary drive wheel
    1. 20" diameter
    2. Solid laminated wood
      1. Dimensionally stable
    3. Axis position is adjustable
  4. Quill Drive
    1. Driven by direct contact with drive wheel
    2. Held in place between drive wheel and roller bearings
    3. 80:1 ratio for quill speed of ~250rpm
  5. References
    1. Peguin wheel
